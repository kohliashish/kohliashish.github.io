About

An aspiring polymath.

Recommendations

My bookshelf.

Here are my top reads that have made a real difference in how I tackle work and life. These books have stuck with me, offering insights I use every day.

"Think Again" by Adam Grant

This book is an easy pick for the top spot and, I'm pretty sure, would be featured in multiple product management blogs, self-help journals, libraries, and bookstores etc. This book challenges our existing beliefs and assumptions about knowledge and emphasizes on unlearning and rethinking. What I love about Adam Grant books is the sheer amount of relevant real-world examples that are included in these books, and Think Again is no exception.

"How to write one song" by Jeff Tweedy

Although the author's original intention with this book was catered to people who are into song writing and other creative pursuits, I think we can learn a lot from this book about how to approach everyday things in life. This short ~150ish pages book can be a great weekend read and gives some really good strategies about how to - Overcome Self-Doubt, Importance of routine & persistence, Embracing imperfections in any pursuit, and Cultivating Curiosity.

"Shantaram" by Gregory David Roberts

I learnt about this classic book very recently during a plane conversation. Although this book is traditionally placed in the fiction category, I feel "Shantaram" helped me with my personal growth in more ways I can imagine. This book teaches us about How to be resilient in the face of adversity, and inculcating empathy by finding common humanity in diverse cultures and backgrounds. If this sounds too boring, just pick the book up for a fun and exciting story! This is part one of a two part book series but I'm yet to get my hands on the next one.
